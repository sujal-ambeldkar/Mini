Common Fungal diseases

Tomato blight

Tomato blight is a devastating fungal disease that affects tomato plants. It spreads rapidly, causing wilting, brown spots, and reduced crop yields.

Rhizoctonia rots

Rhizoctonia rots are caused by the soil-borne fungus Rhizoctonia solani, leading to root decay and plant wilt. This disease affects a wide range of crops, weakening plants and reducing yields

Late Blight

Late blight is a destructive disease caused by the fungus-like organism Phytophthora infestans, affecting tomatoes and potatoes. It leads to rapid plant decay, with dark lesions on leaves, stems, and fruit

Fusarium Wilt

Fusarium wilt is a soil-borne fungal disease caused by Fusarium oxysporum, affecting a wide range of plants. It causes yellowing, wilting, and stunted growth due to the blockage of water and nutrients

Downy Mildew

Downy mildew is a fungal-like disease caused by oomycetes, affecting a variety of crops, including cucumbers and grapes. It leads to yellowing of leaves
